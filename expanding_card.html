HIV, or Human Immunodeficiency Virus, is an incurable sexually transmitted virus that can lead to Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS). Initial symptoms of HIV may mimic those of a cold or flu, such as night sweats, fever, chills, and enlarged lymph nodes throughout the body. HIV finds and attacks healthy immune cells called CD4. Eventually, when a person's CD4 count becomes low enough, they are considered to have AIDS. Although there is no cure for HIV/AIDS, there are many successful anti-viral medications that can halt progression of the virus.
